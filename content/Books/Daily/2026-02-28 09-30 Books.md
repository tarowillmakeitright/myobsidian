## Newly released / newer picks

1. **Nexus: A Brief History of Information Networks from the Stone Age to AI** — *Yuval Noah Harari* (2024)  
   **Why it matters now:** A timely macro-history of information systems and power that frames current AI governance debates in a way that’s useful for both technologists and policy-minded readers.  
   **Reference:** https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/719746/nexus-by-yuval-noah-harari/

2. **The Singularity Is Nearer: When We Merge with AI** — *Ray Kurzweil* (2024)  
   **Why it matters now:** Whether you agree or disagree, this is a core contemporary reference point for accelerationist AI futures, longevity narratives, and human-computer integration claims.  
   **Reference:** https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/749150/the-singularity-is-nearer-by-ray-kurzweil/

3. **Service Model** — *Adrian Tchaikovsky* (2024)  
   **Why it matters now:** Sharp near-future/robotic-society satire that resonates with today’s “agentic AI” discourse and questions of labor, autonomy, and machine governance.  
   **Reference:** https://us.macmillan.com/books/9781250290290/servicemodel

## Legacy / classic must-reads

4. **Neuromancer** — *William Gibson* (1984)  
   **Why it matters now:** Still the foundational cyberpunk text for networked power, hackers, AI agency, and corporate sovereignty—the genre DNA behind much modern tech culture.  
   **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuromancer

5. **Snow Crash** — *Neal Stephenson* (1992)  
   **Why it matters now:** Prescient on virtual worlds, memetic attacks, and techno-linguistic control; keeps paying dividends in discussions around online identity and platform power.  
   **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snow_Crash

6. **The Cuckoo’s Egg** — *Clifford Stoll* (1989)  
   **Why it matters now:** A true-story security classic that teaches patient incident response, adversarial thinking, and operational persistence—timeless skills in modern SOC work.  
   **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Cuckoo%27s_Egg_(book)

7. **The Demon-Haunted World: Science as a Candle in the Dark** — *Carl Sagan* (1995)  
   **Why it matters now:** Essential scientific skepticism toolkit for the AI era: evidence, falsifiability, and resisting hype-driven reasoning.  
   **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Demon-Haunted_World
