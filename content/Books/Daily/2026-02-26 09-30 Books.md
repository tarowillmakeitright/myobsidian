## Legacy / Classic Must-Reads (High Signal)

4) **Neuromancer** — *William Gibson* (SF/cyberpunk)
- **Why it matters now:** Still the foundational “networked future + hacker culture” novel; uncanny relevance to platform power and identity online.
- **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuromancer

5) **The Left Hand of Darkness** — *Ursula K. Le Guin* (SF classic)
- **Why it matters now:** Gold-standard in using SF to examine gender, politics, and anthropology—timeless for thoughtful speculative reading.
- **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Left_Hand_of_Darkness

6) **Gödel, Escher, Bach** — *Douglas Hofstadter* (science/mind)
- **Why it matters now:** Still one of the best bridges between computation, cognition, and meaning—great antidote to shallow AI discourse.
- **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6del,_Escher,_Bach

7) **The Code Book** — *Simon Singh* (cryptography/history)
- **Why it matters now:** Clear mental model for encryption history and principles; practical context for modern privacy/security debates.
- **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Code_Book

8) **The Cuckoo’s Egg** — *Cliff Stoll* (hacker/security memoir)
- **Why it matters now:** Classic incident-response narrative; teaches attacker tradecraft, patience, and investigation discipline better than most modern summaries.
- **Reference:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Cuckoo%27s_Egg_(book)
